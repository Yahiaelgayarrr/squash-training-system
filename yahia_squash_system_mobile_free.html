The 5 rules that override everything
Rule 1 — Tendons lag 6 weeks behind cardio. Never let perceived fitness drive loading before tendons are ready. Achilles, patellar, adductor injuries = 3–6 months off court.
Rule 2 — Every block ends with a deload. Cut volume 35–40%, keep intensity. Adaptation happens during recovery, not the hard weeks.
Rule 3 — Speed and power always first in any session. Never train explosiveness under fatigue. Power → Strength → Accessory. Always.
Rule 4 — Fri/Sat stay light. You have a 4-hour course both days. Post-course heavy training = overtraining. Prehab, swim, short speed only.
Rule 5 — Pain is a stop signal, not something to push through. Consistent joint pain during braking, lunging, or overhead means you need to adjust load, not push harder.
