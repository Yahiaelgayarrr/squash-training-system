MONTH 1 — REBUILD & TISSUE ARMOUR

Weeks 2–5. You have zero fitness base. Your tendons have not trained in a year. Your ankle ligaments are permanently structurally different after grade-3. This month exists to make your body SAFE to train hard later. RPE cap: 6–7 on everything. No ego. No pushing through pain. Every session is a technique session.

Month goals
  • Relearn all movement patterns without injury
  • Establish the 7-day training habit
  • Ankle rehab phase 1 complete
  • Aerobic base started (Z2 adaptation begins)
  • Morning mental routine automatic
  • First ghosting sessions — technical only
Month focus areas
  • Physical: Movement quality, tendons, mobility
  • Court: Footwork patterns, split-step habit, T-position
  • Mental: Box breathing, intention setting, journaling
  • Study: Watch Ali Farag footwork, basic squash tactics
  • Nutrition: Creatine starts NOW, protein targets
  • Sleep: Establish consistent sleep/wake times
Month 1 red lines
  • RPE NEVER above 7 on any exercise this month
  • Zero single-leg plyometrics — ankle not ready
  • No conditioning ghosting — technical only
  • No matches, no competitive play
  • Stop any session if ankle pain hits 2/5
  • Nordic curls: 2×3 ONLY — do not add more

Week 2 focus: Your body is sore from week 1. That's normal. This week your goal is consistency — hit every session, RPE 6 maximum, technique perfect. Volume is low intentionally. You are teaching your nervous system the patterns, not stressing it.

SUNDAY Gym A — Lower body foundation + bilateral plyos Gym ▼

Duration: 55 min · Intensity: RPE 6–7 max · What this week is: Relearn the patterns. Every rep is a technique rep. If anything hurts (sharp, not burn), stop it. Log your session after.

Warm-up

— sets · 10 min · Location: gym floor
  • 5 min easy bike or rower
  • Ankle alphabet (draw A–Z with each foot while seated) × 1 full alphabet each foot
  • Hip 90/90 mobility × 45s each side
  • Glute bridge activation × 10 reps
  • Dead bug × 6 each side, slow
  • Bodyweight split squat × 5 each side — test ankle

Pogo hops (bilateral) Level 1 plyo

3 sets × 15 hops · 60s rest · Ankle spring only
WHY: This is your first plyometric work. Minimal knee bend — you are training ankle tendon stiffness, the same spring mechanism used in every court push-off. Bilateral only until ankle confirms stability.
  • Land on ball of foot — NOT heel
  • Minimal knee bend — the load goes through the ankle tendon
  • Consistent rhythm — like a metronome
  • If ankle pain above 1/5, stop and do stationary calf raises instead

Leg press (machine)

3 sets × 10 reps · 90s rest · Tempo: 3-1-1
WHY: Primary quad builder. The machine controls movement safely while your tendons adapt. 3-second eccentric builds tendon stiffness — this matters more than the weight right now.
  • Full range of motion — all the way down
  • Feet shoulder-width, toes 15° out
  • Count 3 seconds lowering — do not rush it
  • Drive through heels, not toes
  • Weight: start light enough that form is perfect on every rep

DB Split squat (goblet — 2 DBs at chest)

3 sets × 8 reps/side · 90s rest · Tempo: 3-1-1
WHY: This is the most squash-specific gym exercise. Every shot in squash is hit from a lunge position. You are building the exact strength needed. Front heel must NEVER lift — that is your red line.
  • Front foot far enough forward — lunge depth matters
  • Front heel stays flat — if it lifts, move front foot further forward
  • 3 seconds down, 1 second pause at bottom — feel the stretch
  • Keep torso upright — do not lean excessively forward
  • Start with light DBs (5–10kg) — this is week 2

Glute bridge (BW or small DB on hips)

3 sets × 12 reps · 60s rest
  • Pause 2 seconds at top
  • Drive through heels — not toes
  • Squeeze glutes hard at top — do not use lower back

Seated calf raise (machine or DB on knee)

3 sets × 15 reps · 60s rest · Tempo: 3-0-1
WHY: Soleus loading = Achilles tendon health. Bent knee specifically loads the soleus — the primary Achilles tendon muscle. Critical with your ankle history. Start this now and never stop.
  • 3 seconds lowering — this is the medicine
  • Full range up AND down
  • Feel the stretch at the bottom of every rep

Pallof press (cable or band)

3 sets × 10 reps/side · 45s rest
  • Press out — hold 1 second — return
  • Zero trunk rotation — that IS the exercise
  • Stand perpendicular to cable/band anchor

Dead bug

3 sets × 8 reps/side · 45s rest · Slow
  • Lower back GLUED to floor — if it lifts, reduce range of motion
  • Opposite arm + leg extend simultaneously
  • Exhale as you extend the limbs
🧠 Mental training — Week 2 · Foundation Phase · Sunday

Before session (5 min): Box breathing × 5 cycles (inhale 4s → hold 2s → exhale 6s). Then state today's intention out loud: "Today I rebuild my foundation. Every rep is technique." This is not optional. Start this habit now — you are programming your brain for competition calmness.

MONDAY Aerobic Base — Zone 2 Pool ▼

Duration: 35 min total · Intensity: RPE 3–4 · Key rule: If you cannot hold a conversation, you are going too hard. Slow down. This is Zone 2 — the most underrated training quality in squash.

Easy pool swim — any stroke

1 continuous session · 30 min · Nose-breathing pace
WHY: Pool is mandatory in Block 1 because of your ankle. Zero impact. But the aerobic adaptation is identical to running Z2. Zone 2 builds mitochondrial density and capillarisation — this is the base that allows PCr resynthesis between rallies in game 5. Every boring minute of this now = faster first step later.
  • Nose-breathing throughout — if you must breathe through your mouth, you are going too hard
  • Front crawl or breaststroke — whatever allows easy breathing
  • Heart rate ~120–140 bpm (conversation pace)
  • Do not look at the clock — focus on breathing rhythm

Poolside mobility

1 round · 5 min
  • Calf stretch 45s each side
  • Hip flexor stretch 45s each side
  • Ankle circles 15 each direction, each foot
  • Thoracic rotation 10 each side
🧠 Mental training — Monday · Mindful movement

During swim: Every 5 minutes, scan one body sensation: breathing rhythm → arm pull → kick → body position. When your mind wanders to anything else, gently return to the sensation. This is moving meditation. It trains present-moment focus — the foundation of all in-match concentration.

TUESDAY Gym B — Upper body + Posterior chain + Nordic curls Gym ▼

Duration: 55 min · Intensity: RPE 6–7 · CRITICAL: Nordic curls start TODAY. 2 sets of 3 reps ONLY. Lower as slowly as you can. This exercise reduces hamstring injury by 57–70%. The DOMS in 48 hours will be significant — this is normal. Do NOT add more reps because it felt easy.

Warm-up

— · 8 min
  • 5 min easy rower
  • Band pull-aparts × 15
  • Shoulder ER band × 10 each side
  • Hip hinge drill × 8 (push hips back, feel hamstring)
  • Single-leg calf raise × 8 each side, slow

DB Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

3 sets × 10 reps · 90s rest · Tempo: 3-0-1
WHY: Trains the hamstring in its lengthened position — exactly when it tears during sprinting and COD. The 3-second eccentric is where the adaptation happens. This is the most important posterior chain exercise for squash injury prevention.
  • Hinge at the hip — push hips BACK first, not down
  • Soft bend in knees — this is NOT a squat
  • 3 seconds lowering — feel the hamstring stretch at bottom
  • Back stays neutral — do not round the lower back
  • Start light enough to feel the hamstrings on every rep

Nordic hamstring curl Week 2 dose ONLY

2 sets × 3 reps · 90s rest · 4–5 second lowering
WHY: The single most evidence-based injury prevention exercise in sport. 57–70% reduction in hamstring injuries across all sports. You start at 2×3 because even this tiny dose will cause 48-hour DOMS. Week by week, you will add reps. This is a 16-week progression to 3×8 full unassisted.
  • Set up: knees on a mat, ankles hooked under a bench edge or heavy DB
  • Arms crossed on chest or hands ready to catch yourself
  • Lower AS SLOWLY AS POSSIBLE — 4–5 full seconds
  • Catch yourself at 45° angle with your hands — then reset
  • DO NOT add reps this week regardless of how it feels

Pull-up (or assisted pull-up machine)

3 sets × max clean reps (minimum 1) · 2 min rest
WHY: Lat strength, scapular control, bicep strength. Pulling volume must always exceed pressing volume for squash players — the swing is an internal rotation-dominant motion. Pull-ups counteract that.
  • Dead hang start — full arm extension at bottom
  • Pull your CHEST toward the bar — not your chin
  • 2-second controlled lowering
  • If you cannot do 1 pull-up: use assisted machine, or do lat pulldown until you can

Cable face pull

3 sets × 15 reps · 60s rest · Slow and controlled
  • Pull to nose height — elbows flare HIGH and WIDE at end
  • Slow controlled movement — no momentum
  • Light weight — this is health work, not strength work

Landmine press (or kneeling DB press)

3 sets × 8 reps/side · 75s rest
  • Kneeling position — this adds trunk stability demand
  • Press at 45° angle — shoulder-friendly
  • Do not arch the lower back

Soleus isometric (seated, knee bent 90°)

3 sets × 35s each side · 30s rest
WHY: Most specific Achilles tendon protection exercise. Knee bent = loads soleus specifically. Isometric at this angle is the gold standard for Achilles tendon health. Non-negotiable for your ankle history.
  • Seated, knee bent ~90°
  • Push ball of foot into floor with maximum force
  • Hold 35 seconds — do not reduce effort
  • Progress by adding a DB on your knee over weeks
🧠 Mental training — Tuesday · Post-session journal

After session (10 min): Write 3 things: (1) What went well physically or mentally today. (2) One specific thing to improve. (3) One sentence: "I am building the kind of player who..." This identity reinforcement is how champions build self-belief from evidence, not from wishful thinking.

WEDNESDAY Court — Technical footwork and movement quality Squash Court ▼

Duration: 45 min · Intensity: RPE 4–5 · Mindset: This is a SKILL session. Not fitness. Not conditioning. Skill. Slow deliberate movement. Perfect footwork patterns. Your brain has 17 years of squash memory — reconnect to it safely. Wear ankle brace.

Movement preparation

— · 10 min
  • Ankle alphabet on court × 1 each foot
  • Lunge matrix — forward, diagonal, lateral, crossover — 5 reps each direction
  • Easy 6-corner walk × 3 — walk to each corner slowly, split-step back to T
  • 3 × slow build-up runs (30%, 50%, 70%) across the court

Split-step habit drill

10 min dedicated practice
WHY: The split-step is the single most important footwork habit in squash. Elite players do it on 95%+ of opponent shots. It pre-loads the legs for explosive first-step reaction. If you are not doing it automatically, you are slow before the ball is even hit.
  • Stand on the T. Walk to a corner. Walk back. Every time you touch the T: jump into a small split-step landing
  • Both feet land simultaneously — shoulder-width
  • Slight crouch on landing — weight on balls of feet
  • Do this 50 times minimum. It must become reflex.
  • Combine with 3-ball pattern: T → front right → T (split) → back left → T (split)

Technical ghost — 6 corners, slow

4 sets × 90s on / 90s rest · RPE 4 only
WHY: You are reconnecting your 17 years of motor memory to your current body. Slow deliberate movement = precise neural encoding. Fast sloppy movement encodes sloppy patterns. Speed comes later — quality comes now.
  • Move to each corner in sequence: front right → back right → back left → front left → repeat
  • At each corner: shadow a proper shot (racquet back, weight transfer, full lunge)
  • Split-step every time you return to T
  • SLOW — this is not conditioning. Stop and reset if movement feels rushed.
  • Focus cue: "Split step. Lunge. Recover." Say it in your head for every single corner.

T-position accuracy drill

3 sets × 8 corners
  • Ghost to a corner. Hit a shadow shot.
  • Recover to T. PAUSE 2 full seconds — confirm you are exactly on the T
  • Move to next corner
  • The pause is mandatory — it builds the habit of T-accuracy under fatigue later
🧠 Mental training — Wednesday · Single-cue focus

During ghosting: Pick ONE technical cue: "Split step" or "Lunge depth" or "Recover to T." Focus ONLY on that cue for the entire session. Every time your mind wanders to anything else — the scoreboard, how tired you are, anything — return to that one cue. This is the foundation of all squash concentration training.

THURSDAY Prehab + Ankle Rehab Gym Pool (optional after) ▼

Duration: 30 min · Intensity: Easy · Mindset: This is the session that keeps you on court for the other 6 days. Never skip it. Never rush it. The ankle exercises are the most important part. Take 3 slow breaths between exercises — prehab should feel therapeutic.

Ankle alphabet — active ROM ANKLE

2 sets · A–Z each foot · Slow and deliberate
WHY: Restores full talocrural joint ROM after grade-3 sprain. Grade-3 = complete ligament tear. The joint has scar tissue restriction. This actively mobilises every direction before loading.
  • Seated, foot off floor
  • Write A through Z slowly — full range in every direction
  • You should feel resistance in some directions — that is the scar tissue. Work through it gently.

Single-leg balance — Phase 1 ANKLE

3 sets × 30s each foot · Eyes OPEN only this week
WHY: Grade-3 sprains destroy ankle mechanoreceptors — the sensors that tell your brain where your ankle is in space. Balance training rebuilds this neural feedback system. Without it, re-injury rate is very high. Research: balance training reduces ankle sprain recurrence ~46%.
  • Soft knee — never locked
  • Focus on a fixed point on the wall
  • If wobbling significantly, touch a wall gently — do not fall
  • Goal: 30 seconds stable, eyes open

Peroneal eccentric — band ANKLE

3 sets × 12 reps each foot · 30s rest · Slow
WHY: Grade-3 sprains damage the peroneal tendons — your lateral ankle stabilisers. This exercise specifically rebuilds them eccentrically. Without peroneal strength, lateral ankle stability is gone and every hard COD is a re-injury risk.
  • Band around the front of your foot, anchor to the side
  • Evert foot outward (sole faces sideways) against the band
  • Control the return SLOWLY — that is the eccentric
  • Feel the outside of your lower leg working

Eccentric calf raise on step ANKLE

3 sets × 12 reps · 45s rest · Bilateral this month
  • Rise up on TWO feet at top
  • Lower on ONE foot — 3–5 second slow descent
  • If ankle pain above 2/5: bilateral lowering only
  • Full heel drop below step level at bottom

Shoulder prehab circuit

2 rounds · Short rest between exercises
  • Band ER × 15 each side
  • Face pull × 15
  • Prone Y raise × 10
  • Band pull-apart × 15

Copenhagen plank (groin + adductor)

2 sets × 20s each side · Short lever (lower knee on bench)
  • Top leg on bench, bottom leg free or barely touching floor
  • Hips square — do not let one side drop
  • Start at 20 seconds — progress 5s each week

Hip 90/90 stretch + active reps

2 sets · 45s hold each side + 8 active reps
  • Passive hold first — feel the hip
  • Then actively: lift front shin, hold 3s, lower
  • This improves hip rotation needed for lunge depth and shot production
🧠 Mental training — Thursday · Breathing as prehab

Throughout all prehab exercises: Breathe intentionally — 4s inhale through nose, 2s hold, 6s exhale through mouth. Do this for EVERY SINGLE exercise. The prehab session becomes a breathing meditation. This lowers cortisol, improves parasympathetic recovery, and is used by elite athletes pre-competition. You are making it automatic now.

FRIDAY Pool recovery — Block 1 only Swimming Pool ▼

Duration: 25–30 min · Intensity: Z1 recovery · Note: In Block 1 (months 1–2), Friday is active recovery only. Track sprints begin month 3. Your ankle is still adapting. Pool = perfect zero-impact recovery while also building aerobic base.

Easy pool swim

1 session · 20–25 min · Z1 pace (RPE 2–3)
  • Even easier than Monday — this is pure recovery
  • Focus on long smooth strokes
  • If legs are sore: water walking or light kicking with board
  • You should feel BETTER leaving than when you arrived

Aqua jogging (shallow end, optional)

5–10 min
WHY: Water buoyancy reduces body weight by ~50% — this is partial weight-bearing ankle loading. Perfect progressive ankle rehab at this stage.
  • Run in place or across the shallow end
  • Natural running gait — no exaggeration
  • This loads the ankle progressively without impact
🧠 Mental training — Friday · Process focus for next week

After pool (5 min poolside): Write one SMART goal for next week's training. Specific + Measurable + Achievable. Example: "I will complete the split-step on every T return in Wednesday's ghosting session." This goal-setting practice is documented to improve training consistency and confidence by directing your attention to controllable actions.

(4) Write your 3 confidence anchors — real moments this week where you felt capable. (5) Rate your mental performance 1–10. Stay at 2×3 for 2 full weeks before progressing. If you are too sore to walk down stairs, you started too heavy — reduce the angle next session.

Week 4 — load week: This is the last loading week before deload. Push RPE to 7–7.5 on main lifts. Nordic curls progress to 2×4. Ghosting intensity can go to 70% pace (still technical, not conditioning). Balance: 40s each side. Friday: attempt first track session — 4×30m easy run (not sprint).

Week 4 progressions
  • Leg press: Push to RPE 7.5 — last 2 reps should be genuinely challenging
  • DB split squat: Add another 2.5kg if form held last week
  • Nordic curls: Progress to 2×4 reps (first progression)

Week 5 — DELOAD. Drop ALL working sets by 1 (e.g., 3×10 → 2×10). Keep the same weights and RPE. Shorter sessions. This is not a rest week — it is a low-stress repetition of the patterns. The adaptation from weeks 2–4 happens THIS week. Do not be tempted to add anything.

Deload rules
  • All working sets reduced by 1 (minimum 2 sets per exercise)
  • Same weight/intensity as last week — do not go lighter
  • No Nordic curls this week
  • Ghosting: 3 sets only, at 60% pace
  • Z2 swim: 25 min
  • Add 10 min extra sleep this week if possible
  • Test benchmark: How many pull-ups can you do? How far does a broad jump land? Note these numbers.

End of Month 1 benchmark check: DB split squat with 15kg DB × 8 clean reps each side. Single-leg balance 30s eyes open. Pogo hops 3×15 consistent rhythm. 6-corner ghosting 3×90s at 70% pace with consistent split-step. Nordic curls 2×4 (slow). If you hit these — you are ready for Month 2.

SUNDAY Gym A — Strength + Box jump introduction Gym ▼

Box jump (bilateral — STEP DOWN) New — Level 2 plyo

4 sets × 4 reps · 2 min rest · Maximum height
WHY: You are now ready for box jumps. The step-down instruction is critical — jumping off the box increases landing impact significantly. At this stage your tendons are not ready for that. Step down = concentric power without excessive eccentric stress.
  • Arms swing back, then drive up aggressively
  • Land softly — absorb with hips and knees
  • STEP DOWN — do not jump off the box
  • Full reset between reps — this is power, not conditioning
  • Start with 30cm box — increase height when landing is completely controlled

Leg press HEAVY

4 sets × 6–8 reps · 2–3 min rest · Tempo 3-0-X
  • Log the weight. It MUST increase this block.
  • Last 2 reps should feel genuinely hard (RPE 8)
  • 3 seconds down, drive up as fast as possible
  • Rest fully — 2.5 min minimum between sets

DB RFESS — rear foot elevated split squat Primary exercise

4 sets × 6 reps/side · 2 min rest · Tempo 3-1-X
WHY: This replaces the split squat this month. More challenging, more specific. Rear foot on bench changes the balance and quad demand entirely. This is the most squash-specific gym exercise that exists. Learn it well now — you will be doing it for years.
  • Rear foot elevated on bench — not too high (15–30cm)
  • Front foot far enough forward that front shin is vertical at the bottom
  • 3s eccentric — own the bottom position for 1s
  • Drive explosively up — this is the squash push-off
  • Front heel NEVER lifts — if it does, move front foot further forward
  • Start: just DBs in each hand at sides (15–20kg each)

Hip thrust (machine or DB on hips)

3 sets × 10 reps · 75s rest
  • Full hip extension at top — pause 1s
  • Drive through heels — not toes
  • Ribs down, chin tucked — do not hyperextend lower back

Lateral lunge (DB)

3 sets × 8 reps/side · 75s rest · Tempo 3-2-1
WHY: Squash is a laterally dominant sport. Lateral lunge loads the adductors and quads in the wide positions your body takes on court. This prevents groin injuries and builds lateral force production.
  • Step directly to the side — lunge as deep as your hip allows
  • 3s into the position, 2s hold — feel adductors under load
  • Drive back out through mid-foot
  • Keep non-lunging leg straight

Standing calf raise (machine or DB)

4 sets × 10 reps · 60s rest · Heavy — 3s lowering
  • Heavy — should struggle by rep 8
  • Straight knee = gastrocnemius = push-off power
  • 3s lowering — this is where the adaptation is

Duration: 40 min total · Intensity: Hard on hard reps · Science: 10s hard / 20s easy format. Hard phase: glycolytic + ATP-PCr system. 20s rest = only ~60% PCr recovery → forces glycolytic adaptation → raises lactate threshold over weeks → you sustain higher intensity in games 3–5.

Warm-up

10 min
  • 8 min easy jog or swim
  • 4 × 20m accelerations at 70%
  • Dynamic mobility: leg swings, hip openers

10s hard / 20s easy intervals

3 sets × 10 reps · 3 min rest between sets
WHY: This format is specifically effective for squash aerobic power. The 10s interval matches rally energy demands. The 20s rest matches between-rally rest. You are training the exact energy system ratio of a squash match.
  • Hard = 90% max effort — clean mechanics, explosive push
  • Easy = walk or very slow jog only
  • Track: sprint the straights, walk the curve
  • Pool: sprint 1 length, easy 1 length
  • Both legs should be burning by rep 8–10
  • If form breaks down before rep 10: stop the set

Easy cool-down

8 min
  • Easy jog or swim
  • Bring HR below 120 before leaving

WHY: Every squash first step is 94% ATP-PCr energy. 5m sprint depletes PCr ~40%. 3 min rest = >90% PCr restored. You are training alactic power AND aerobic PCr resynthesis rate — the foundation of game-5 first-step speed.

First RSA session — exact protocol

RSA block (Week 12 introduction)

2 sets × 6 reps · 25s rest between reps · 4 min between sets
WHY: RSA training is the most sport-specific conditioning for squash. It directly trains the ATP-PCr + aerobic interface — the system that determines whether your first step in rally 60 of game 5 is the same quality as rally 1 of game 1. This is what separates top-50 from top-5.
